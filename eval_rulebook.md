# Pandemic Rulebook

## Overview

In Pandemic, two to four players work together as members of a disease control team. The goal is to discover cures for four deadly diseases before they spread out of control. Players lose if too many outbreaks occur, if the disease cube supply runs out, or if the player card deck is exhausted.

## Components

- 1 game board
- 7 role cards and matching pawns
- 6 research station markers
- 96 disease cubes (24 each in blue, yellow, black, and red)
- 48 infection cards
- 4 reference cards
- 59 player cards (48 city cards, 6 epidemic cards, 5 event cards)
- 4 cure markers and 1 eradication marker
- 1 infection rate marker
- 1 outbreak marker

## Setup

Place the board in the center of the table. Each player draws a role card and takes the corresponding pawn.

Separate the epidemic cards from the player deck. Divide the remaining player cards into equal piles based on difficulty level:
- Introductory (4 Epidemic cards)
- Standard (5 Epidemic cards)
- Heroic (6 Epidemic cards)

Shuffle one epidemic card into each pile, then stack the piles to form the player deck. Players are dealt cards based on player count: 4 players receive 2 cards each, 3 players receive 3 cards each, 2 players receive 4 cards each.

Place the research station markers near the board. One research station begins in Atlanta. Set the infection rate marker on the "2" space and the outbreak marker on the "0" space.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, you may take up to 4 actions. Actions may be taken in any order and may be repeated.

### Available Actions

**Drive/Ferry** — Move your pawn to a city connected by a white line.

**Direct Flight** — Discard a city card to move your pawn to that city.

**Charter Flight** — Discard the card matching your current city to move to any city on the board.

**Shuttle Flight** — Move between any two cities that each have a research station.

**Build a Research Station** — Discard the card matching your current city to build a research station there.

**Treat Disease** — Remove one disease cube of any color from your current city. If a disease has been cured, remove all cubes of that color from the city instead.

**Share Knowledge** — Give or take a city card from another player in the same city. Both players must agree.

**Discover a Cure** — At a research station, discard 5 cards of the same color to cure the disease of that color.

## Drawing Player Cards

After taking your 4 actions, draw 2 player cards from the top of the player deck. If you draw an epidemic card, follow the epidemic procedure immediately.

### Epidemic Procedure

1. **Increase** — Move the infection rate marker one space to the right.
2. **Infect** — Draw the bottom card of the infection deck and place 3 disease cubes on that city.
3. **Intensify** — Shuffle the infection discard pile and place it on top of the infection deck.

If the player card deck runs out and you need to draw, the players immediately lose the game.

<page>5</page>
## Infection Phase

After drawing player cards, infect cities. Draw infection cards equal to the current infection rate (shown on the track: 2, 2, 2, 3, 3, 4, 4).

Place one disease cube of the matching color on each city drawn. If a city already has 3 disease cubes of that color when it would receive another, an outbreak occurs instead.

### Outbreaks

When a city already has 3 disease cubes of one color and would receive another cube:
- Do not place a cube in that city.
- Move the outbreak marker one space forward on the track.
- Place one cube of that color in each city connected to the outbreak city.

If any connected city also already has 3 cubes, a chain reaction outbreak occurs — that city also triggers an outbreak (but cities that already triggered outbreaks this turn do not trigger a second time).

If the outbreak marker reaches the last space on the track (8 outbreaks), the players immediately lose.

## Win and Lose Conditions

### Winning

Players win immediately when all 4 diseases are cured, regardless of how many disease cubes remain on the board or how many eradications have occurred.

### Losing

Players lose immediately if any of the following occur:
- The player card deck runs out and a player needs to draw.
- The outbreak marker reaches the last space (8 outbreaks).
- A disease cube must be placed but there are no cubes of that color remaining in the supply.

## Role Abilities

### Medic

The Medic removes **all** disease cubes of one color when treating a disease, using only 1 action. When a disease is cured, the Medic automatically removes all cubes of that color in any city they enter or are already in.

### Scientist

The Scientist needs only **4** cards of the same color (instead of 5) to discover a cure.

### Researcher

The Researcher may give any city card to another player in the same city during the Share Knowledge action — the card does not need to match the current city.

### Operations Expert

The Operations Expert may build a research station in their current city without discarding the matching city card. Once per turn, they may move from a research station to any city by discarding any city card.

### Dispatcher

The Dispatcher may move any other player's pawn to a city containing another pawn as an action. The Dispatcher may also spend their actions to move another player's pawn as if it were their own.

### Quarantine Specialist

The Quarantine Specialist prevents disease cube placement and outbreaks in the city they are currently in **and all cities directly connected to it**.

### Contingency Planner

The Contingency Planner may take any event card from the infection discard pile and store it on their role card. There is no hand limit for the stored card, and it can be played at any time. When used, the card is removed from the game.

## Research Stations

Research stations are required to discover cures. There can be at most **6 research stations** on the board at one time. If all 6 are already placed and you want to build another, you must first move one of the existing stations to the new location.

## Special Rules: Eradication

When a disease is cured and there are no remaining disease cubes of that color on the board, the disease is eradicated. Flip the cure marker to the eradication side. An eradicated disease cannot spread — no cubes of that color are placed during the infection phase, and epidemic cards for eradicated diseases are ignored.

Eradication is not required to win; curing all 4 diseases is sufficient.
